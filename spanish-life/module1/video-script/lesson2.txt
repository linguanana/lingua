********** EN Script (talking script) **********
{{pause=500}}¿Cómo estás?{{pause=500}} is your casual go-to for friends and family. {{pause=1000}} It’s based on the informal tú and is used with people you know well. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}¿Cómo está?{{pause=500}} also means “How are you?” but it's for formal situations. {{pause=1000}} You use this with strangers, your professor, or your boss. {{pause=1000}} This phrase shows respect and politeness. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}¿Qué tal?{{pause=500}} is super relaxed, like saying “How’s it going?” or “What’s up?” {{pause=1000}} It's perfect for almost any casual situation and a great all-purpose phrase. {{pause=1000}}

Now, here’s a cool nuance! When you ask {{pause=500}}¿Todo bien?{{pause=500}}
it’s a quick and cheerful way to greet someone you know, like when you pass a friend in the hallway. {{pause=1000}}

But if you use {{pause=500}}¿Estás bien?{{pause=500}}, it’s because you are genuinely worried and want to know if they are okay.
{{pause=1000}} See the difference? {{pause=1000}}

********** EN Script (talking script) **********
When someone asks how you are, a simple {{pause=500}}Estoy bien{{pause=500}} or {{pause=500}}Bien{{pause=500}}
is always a safe and natural response. {{pause=1000}}

If you're feeling especially good, you can use something more energetic like
{{pause=500}}Muy bien{{pause=500}} or even {{pause=500}}¡Estoy genial!{{pause=500}}. {{pause=1000}}

If you're just feeling so-so, you can say {{pause=500}}Más o menos{{pause=500}}. {{pause=1000}}
It’s a very honest and useful answer. {{pause=1000}}

And finally — {{pause=500}}Estoy bien, ¡gracias! ¿Y tú?{{pause=500}}
This is a full, polite response that also remembers to ask the other person how they are doing. {{pause=500}}
This is a very important cultural habit in Spanish-speaking countries.
Always remember to ask the question back! {{pause=1000}}
