This means “Good morning.” {{pause=500}}

{{pause=500}} ¡Buenas tardes! {{pause=500}}
This means “Good afternoon.” {{pause=500}}
You use this one from the afternoon until sunset — a very common greeting. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=500}} ¡Buenas noches! {{pause=500}}
This one means “Good night” and it’s a bit special. {{pause=500}}
You can use it as a greeting when you arrive somewhere in the evening, {{pause=500}}
and you also use it as a farewell right before someone goes to bed. {{pause=500}}
It's a beautiful, gentle way to say goodnight.  {{pause=1000}}

********** EN Script (talking script) **********
{{pause=1000}}Adiós {{pause=500}} is a very common goodbye. {{pause=500}}

It’s polite and neutral, so you can use it with anyone. {{pause=1000}}
But be careful, {{pause=500}} it can sometimes sound a little formal or final, {{pause=500}}
especially if you’re saying goodbye to a good friend. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=1000}}So you’ll hear it a lot in formal settings, like in a store or at the end of a meeting.{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Chao{{pause=500}} is the informal version — perfect with friends. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Hasta luego{{pause=500}} — you’ll see this person again today. {{pause=1000}}
It’s a very popular way to say, “Catch you later!” {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Hasta más tarde{{pause=500}}  is a little more specific, and you'll often use it if you know you’re going to see them later in the day. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Hasta mañana {{pause=500}}  is for when you’ll see someone tomorrow. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Hasta la próxima {{pause=500}} — “until next time,” and it’s open-ended. {{pause=500}}
You don’t know when, but you’ll meet again. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Hasta pronto{{pause=500}} is a warm and friendly way to say “see you soon.” {{pause=1000}}
You use it when you hope — or expect — to see the person soon. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=500}} Nos vemos{{pause=500}}  — it means “See you.” {{pause=1000}}
It’s short, friendly, and used a lot. {{pause=1000}}
If you want to add when, you can say:
{{pause=500}} Nos vemos mañana{{pause=500}}  — See you tomorrow. {{pause=1000}}
Or {{pause=500}} Nos vemos pronto{{pause=500}} — See you soon. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} Hablamos pronto{{pause=500}}  — for calls or messages. Like “Talk soon!” {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}} ¡Que tengas un buen día!{{pause=500}} — say it when someone’s heading out for the day. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=500}} ¡Que pases una buena tarde!{{pause=500}}  — a polite way to say “Have a good afternoon/evening.” {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=500}} ¡Que descanses!{{pause=500}}  — this is the perfect phrase to say to someone who is going to sleep. {{pause=1000}}
