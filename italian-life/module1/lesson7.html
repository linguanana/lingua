✅ Level 1 (A0): Introducing Yourself and Others

📗 Key Phrases: To Be (Essere)

Io sono... – I am... (我是...)
Sono... – I'm... (我是... - *subject omitted*)
Tu sei... – You are... (你是...)
Sei...? – Are you...? (你是...嗎？)

🎯 Dialogue / Practice

🧸 A: Sei Marco?
→ Are you Marco? (你是馬可嗎？)

👨 B: Sì, sono Marco.
→ Yes, I'm Marco. (對，我是馬可)

🧸 A: E tu?
→ And you? (那你呢？)

👩 B: Sono Laura.
→ I'm Laura. (我是勞拉)

📌 Tip

• In Italian, people often drop io because the verb already shows who’s speaking.
• Example: Io sono Anna → Sono Anna
•（意大利人常省略 io，因為動詞變化已經透露主詞是誰）

💠 "Tu sei" or just "Sei"?>

• Similarly, in a question, you can just use the verb: Sei Marco? (Are you Marco?) instead of Tu sei Marco?

✅ Level 2 (A0+): Meet the Italian Subject Pronouns

📗 Key Pronouns

io – I（我）
io sono... – I am... (我是...)
tu – you (informal)（你）
tu sei... – You are... (你、非正式單數)
lui – he（他）
lui è... – He is... (他是...)
lei – she（她）
lei è... – She is... (她是...)
Lei – you (formal)（您，正式）
Lei è... – You are... (您、正式單數)
noi – we（我們）
noi siamo... – We are... (我們是...)
voi – you all（你們）
voi siete... – You all are... (你們是...)
loro – they（他們 / 她們）
loro sono... – They are... (他們/她們是...)

🎯 Dialogue / Practice

🧸 A: Tu sei Maria?
→ Are you Maria?（你是瑪麗亞嗎？）

👩 B: Sì, io sono Maria.
→ Yes, I am Maria.（對，我是瑪麗亞）


🧸 A: Sei pronto?
→ Are you ready? (你準備好了嗎？)

👨 B: Sì, sono pronto.
→ Yes, I am ready. (對，我準備好了)

🧸 A: Lui è Luca?
→ Is he Luca?（他是路卡嗎？）

👩 B: Sì, lui è Luca.
→ Yes, he is Luca.（對，他是路卡）


🧸 A: Lei è italiana?
→ Is she Italian? (她是義大利人嗎？)

👨 B: Sì, lei è italiana.
→ Yes, she is Italian. (對，她是義大利人)

🧸 A: Scusi, Lei è il signor Bianchi?
→ Excuse me, are you Mr. Bianchi? (Formal) (打擾了，您是比安奇先生嗎？)

👩 B: Sì, sono io.
→ Yes, it's me. (對，是我)

🧸 A: Noi siamo amici?
→ Are we tourists?（我們是朋友嗎？）

👨‍👩‍👧 B: Sì, siamo amici.
→ Yes, we are tourists.（對，我們是朋友）


🧸 A: Voi siete turisti?
→ Are you all students?（你們是遊客嗎？）

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 B: Sì, siamo turisti
→ Yes, we are students.（對，我們是遊客）


🧸 A: Loro sono americani?
→ Are they American?（他們是美國人嗎？）

👫 B: Sì, sono americani.
→ Yes, they are American.（對，他們是美國人）

📌 Tip

💠 About subject pronouns in Italian

🔹 In Italian, subject pronouns are often dropped because the verb form already shows who’s speaking.
🔹 Example: (Io) sono Laura, (Lui) è Marco.
🔹 Capitalized Lei (formal “you”) is the same as “she” lei — context matters!
🔹（意大利人會省略主詞，因為動詞本身就能看出是誰；Lei 與 lei 要靠語境區分）

✅ Level 3 (A1): Possessive Pronouns

📗 Key Phrases

💠 Masculine Singular / Feminine Singular 💠 il mio / la mia – my + Singular (我的)
il tuo / la tua – your (informal) + Singular (你的)
il suo / la sua – his / her / your (formal) (他/她/ 您的)
il nostro / la nostra – our (我們的)
il vostro / la vostra – your (你們的)
il loro / la loro – their / your (formal) (他們/她們/您們的)
💠 Masculine Plural / Feminine Plural 💠 i miei / le mie – my + Plural (我的)
i tuoi / le tue – your (informal) + Plural(你的)
i suoi / le sue – his / her / your (formal) (他/她/您的)
i nostri / le nostre – our (我們的)
i vostri / le vostre – your (你們的)
i loro / le loro – their / your (formal) (他們/她們/您們的)

🎯 Dialogue / Practice

👧 A: È la tua borsa?
→ Is it your bag?（這是你的包包嗎？）

👩 B: Sì, è la mia borsa.
→ Yes, it’s my bag.（對，是我的包包）


👧 A: Dov’è il suo telefono?
→ Where is his/her phone?（他的/她的手機在哪？）

👩 B: Ecco il suo telefono.
→ Here’s his/her phone.（這是他的/她的手機）


👧 A: È la vostra macchina?
→ Is this your (plural) car?（這是你們的車嗎？）

👨‍👩‍👧 B: Sì, è la nostra macchina.
→ Yes, it’s our car.（對，是我們的車）

👧 A: Sono le loro chiavi?
→ Are these their keys? (這些是他們的鑰匙嗎？)

👫 B: No, non sono le loro chiavi. Sono le mie.
→ No, they aren't their keys. They are mine. (不，那些不是他們的鑰匙。是我的。)

📌 Tip

💠 Gender and Agreement Matter

• In Italian, possessive words agree with the **noun**, not with the person.
• la mia borsa (my bag – feminine)
• il mio libro (my book – masculine)
•（意大利語中，所有格形容詞的性別與所修飾的名詞一致，而不是與說話者一致）

💠 Gender and Number Agreement are Key!

• In Italian, possessive words (like "my," "your") always agree in **gender (masculine/feminine)** and **number (singular/plural)** with the **noun they describe** (the thing being possessed), *not* with the person who owns it. This is a fundamental concept!
• Example:
    la mia borsa (my bag – *borsa* is feminine singular, so *mia* is feminine singular)
    il mio libro (my book – *libro* is masculine singular, so *mio* is masculine singular)
    i miei libri (my books – *libri* is masculine plural, so *miei* is masculine plural)
    le mie borse (my bags – *borse* is feminine plural, so *mie* is feminine plural)
(義大利語中，所有格詞的性別和單複數要與它所修飾的「名詞」（被擁有的東西）一致，而不是與擁有者一致。這是一個非常重要的概念。)

🔹 The Definite Article is (Almost) Always There:
• Possessive adjectives nearly always come with a **definite article** (il, la, i, le).
• Example: il mio libro (my book), la tua casa (your house), i nostri amici (our friends).
(所有格形容詞幾乎總是伴隨著定冠詞使用。)

🔹 Exception: Family Members (Singular)
• You generally **omit the article** when referring to *singular* unadorned family members (e.g., mom, dad, brother, sister).
• Example: mia madre (my mother), tuo padre (your father), sua sorella (his/her sister).
• **BUT:** If the family member is *plural*, or has an adjective, or is *loro*, you use the article!
    Example: i miei genitori (my parents), il mio fratello piccolo (my little brother), il loro padre (their father).
(單數的、不帶形容詞的家庭成員名詞前通常省略冠詞。但如果是複數、帶形容詞或「loro」，則需要使用冠詞。)

🔹 "Suo" vs. "Loro" - Formal "You" Context:
• il suo / la sua... can mean **his, her**, or **your (formal singular)**.
• il loro / la loro... can mean **their**, or **your (formal plural)**.
• Just like with the subject pronoun *Lei*, you need to rely on **context** to understand the meaning when using formal "you" possessives.
(「suo」可以指「他/她的」或「您的」（單數正式）。「loro」可以指「他們的/她們的」或「您們的」（複數正式）。需要依賴語境來區分。)
