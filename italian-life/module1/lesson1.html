📘 Theme: Greetings in everyday Italian
📚 Lessons: 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7

✅ Level 1 (A0): Say Hello!

📗 Key Phrase

Ciao! – Hi / Bye! （嗨／掰）
Buongiorno! – Good morning! （早安／你好）
Buonasera! – Good evening! （晚上好）
Salve! – Hello (formal or neutral) （您好）

🎯 Dialogue


👩 A: Ciao!
→ Hi! （嗨！）

👨 B: Buongiorno!
→ Good morning! （你好！）

👩 A: Salve!
→ Hello (formal) （您好）

👨 B: Buonasera!
→ Good evening! （晚上好）

👩 A: Ciao, a presto!
→ Bye, see you soon! （掰，回頭見！）

🧪 Task or homework

Go to a local café or market and say “Ciao!” or “Buongiorno!” to someone new. Notice how they respond.

📌 Tip

“Ciao” is informal and very common in Italy. （口語用法，適合朋友之間）
“Buongiorno” is polite and used until about 5 p.m. （較正式，通常在下午 5 點前使用）
“Buonasera” is for after 5–6 p.m. （傍晚以後的問候語）
“Salve” is a nice neutral option if you're unsure which to use.
You can use “Ciao” for both greeting and saying goodbye!

✅ Level 2 (A0+): More Greetings & Goodbyes

📗 More Phrases

Buon pomeriggio! – Good afternoon!（午安，用於下午打招呼）
Buonanotte! – Good night, before going to bed（晚安，睡前說，不再見面）
Arrivederci! – Goodbye, not sure when you’ll meet again（正式的再見，下次見時間不定）
A dopo! – See you later, today（待會見，今天內會再見）
A presto! – See you soon, within a few days（很快再見，可能今天或幾天內）
A domani! – See you tomorrow（明天見，很確定是明天）
Piacere! – Nice to meet you, first time（很高興認識你，第一次見面用）
Ciao ciao! – Bye-bye, maybe or maybe not again（掰掰～可愛口吻，不一定會再見）

🧪 Task or homework

Try using different greetings in different situations! Say “Buonanotte” to someone before bed, or “A domani” after school or work.

📌 Tip

“Buonanotte” is only for bedtime! 🌙
“Arrivederci” is more formal than “Ciao”.
“A presto” and “A dopo” are good if you’ll see the person again soon.
“Piacere” is useful when meeting someone for the first time.

✅ Level 3 (A1): More Goodbyes

📗 More Casual & Online Phrases

A dopo! – See you later（待會見，今天內會再見）
A dopo ragazzi! – See you later, guys!（待會見，大家）
A più tardi! – See you later today（晚點見）
Ci vediamo! – See you (in person)
Ci sentiamo! – Talk soon (may not in person)（再聯絡，適用於線上）
Ci sentiamo domani! – Talk to you tomorrow（明天再聯絡）
A prestissimo! – See you very soon（非常快再見，更親密語氣）
Buona serata! – Have a nice evening（祝你今晚愉快）
Buona giornata! – Have a good day（祝你今天愉快）

🧪 Task or homework

Try ending your chats or online calls in Italian! Say “Ci sentiamo” after a message, or “A presto!” after class or a meeting.

📌 Tip

“Ci sentiamo” is often used in messaging or casual speech.
“A prestissimo” adds warmth and closeness – great for friends!
“Buona serata” and “Buona giornata” are polite, useful anytime!
