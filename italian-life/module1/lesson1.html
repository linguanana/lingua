📘 Theme: Greetings in everyday Italian

📚 Lessons: 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7

✅ Level 1 – A0: Say Hello!

📗 Key Phrase

Ciao! – Hi / Bye! （嗨／掰）
Buongiorno! – Good morning! （早安／你好）
Buonasera! – Good evening! （晚上好）
Salve! – Hello (formal or neutral) （您好）

🎯 Dialogue


👩 A: Ciao!
→ Hi! （嗨！）

👨 B: Buongiorno!
→ Good morning! （你好！）

👩 A: Salve!
→ Hello (formal) （您好）

👨 B: Buonasera!
→ Good evening! （晚上好）

👩 A: Ciao, a presto!
→ Bye, see you soon! （掰，回頭見！）

🧪 Task or homework

Go to a local café or market and say “Ciao!” or “Buongiorno!” to someone new. Notice how they respond.

📌 Tip

“Ciao” is informal and very common in Italy. （口語用法，適合朋友之間）
“Buongiorno” is polite and used until about 5 p.m. （較正式，通常在下午 5 點前使用）
“Buonasera” is for after 5–6 p.m. （傍晚以後的問候語）
“Salve” is a nice neutral option if you're unsure which to use.
You can use “Ciao” for both greeting and saying goodbye!
