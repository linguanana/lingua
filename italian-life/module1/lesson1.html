✅ Level 1 (A0): Say Hello!

🔐 Key Phrase

Ciao! – Hi / Bye! （嗨／掰）
Buongiorno! – Good morning! （早安／你好）
Buonasera! – Good evening! （晚上好）
Salve! – Hello (formal or neutral) （您好）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

😄 When: Talking to friends or classmates 跟朋友
Say Ciao! – casual and friendly.（隨意又親切）

👩‍⚕️ When: Not sure what to say 不知道要說什麼
Use Salve! – polite, neutral, good for anyone.（禮貌又中性，適合所有場合）

🕗 When: Morning at a hotel or store 飯店、商店的早上
Say Buongiorno! – polite and common before 5 p.m.（早上常用，很有禮貌）

🧑‍🍳 When: At a clothing store or café 咖啡廳、商店
Say Buongiorno! when you walk in – it’s polite and friendly!（一進門就說，更加分）

🌆 When: Evening at a restaurant or event 傍晚用餐或參加活動
Say Buonasera! – evening greeting after 5–6 p.m.（5 點後改說這個）

👋 When: Leaving a short meeting or chat 離開時說
Say A presto! – see you soon.（待會見）

🙋‍♀️ When: Greeting a group (e.g. in class) 跟一群人打招呼
Say Ciao a tutti! – Hi everyone!（大家好）

🤖 Task or homework

Go to a local café or market and say “Ciao!” or “Buongiorno!” to someone new. Notice how they respond.

📌 Tip

Ciao – is informal and very common in Italy. （口語用法，適合朋友之間）
Buongiorno – is polite and used until about 5 p.m. （較正式，通常在下午 5 點前使用）
Buonasera – is for after 5–6 p.m. （傍晚以後的問候語）
Salve – is a nice neutral option if you're unsure which to use.
You can use Ciao for both greeting and saying goodbye!

💞 Cultural Insight

Ciao: Two Meanings!
In Italy, Ciao means both "Hi" and "Bye"! You use it with friends.

Morning or Evening?
Use Buongiorno in the morning and afternoon. After 4 or 5 p.m., switch to Buonasera. Italians are careful about greetings for different times!

✅ Level 2 (A0+): More Greetings & Goodbyes

🎯 More Phrases


🌞 Early afternoon and want to be polite?
Say Buon pomeriggio! – Good afternoon（午安）

🌙 Heading to bed?
Say Buonanotte! – Good night, before bed（睡前說晚安）

🎩 Leaving formally (e.g., restaurant or meeting)?
Say Arrivederci! – Goodbye (formal)（正式的再見）

⏳ Saying goodbye for now but will meet later today?
Say A dopo! – See you later today（今天稍後見）

⚡ Ending a quick conversation or short visit?
Say A presto! – See you soon（很快再見）

📅 Parting ways until tomorrow?
Say A domani! – See you tomorrow（明天見）

🤝 Meeting someone for the first time?
Say Piacere! – Nice to meet you（初次見面）

👋 Saying bye with a cute or friendly tone?
Say Ciao ciao! – Bye-bye!（可愛口吻的掰掰）

🧪 Task or Homework

Try using these greetings in real life! Say “Buonanotte” before bed, or “A domani” after class or work.

📌 Tip

Buonanotte is only for bedtime 🌙
Arrivederci is more formal than Ciao
A presto and A dopo are good when you’ll see someone again soon
Piacere is for first meetings

💞 Cultural Insight

Piacere: Nice to Meet You!
When you meet someone new, saying Piacere! (Nice to meet you!) is very polite. People often shake hands too.

Arrivederci: Formal Goodbye
Arrivederci is a more formal "goodbye." Use it for people you might not see soon, or when you want to show respect to elders or strangers, like in shops.

✅ Level 3 (A0+): Even More Ways to Say Goodbye

🎯 More Casual & Online Phrases


👋 Wrapping up a conversation?
Say A dopo! – See you later（待會見）

🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Saying goodbye to a group of friends?
Say A dopo ragazzi! – See you later, guys!（大家待會見）

🕒 Meeting again later today?
Say A più tardi! – See you later today（今天稍後見）

🙋‍♂️ Planning to see someone in person?
Say Ci vediamo! – See you in person（到時見）

📆 Scheduled to meet tomorrow?
Say Ci vediamo domani! – See you tomorrow（明天見面）

📞 Will contact again soon?
Say Ci sentiamo! – Talk soon（再聯絡）

📲 Planning to call or message tomorrow?
Say Ci sentiamo domani! – Talk to you tomorrow（明天再聯絡）

⚡ Really soon – like within the hour?
Say A prestissimo! – See you very soon（非常快再見）

🌆 Saying goodbye in the evening?
Say Buona serata! – Have a nice evening（祝你今晚愉快）

🌞 Saying goodbye in the morning or early afternoon?
Say Buona giornata! – Have a good day（祝你今天愉快）

🔁 Ending for now, but you’ll meet again?
Say Alla prossima! – See you next time（下次見）

📅 Meeting again next week?
Say Alla prossima settimana! – See you next week（下週見）

🕰️ No date planned, but you'll reconnect later?
Say Alla prossima volta! – Until next time（等下次見面）

🤖 Task or Homework

Try ending a call or message in Italian! Say “Ci sentiamo” online, or “A presto!” after a class or meeting.

📌 Tip

Ci vediamo is used when you expect to meet in person
Ci sentiamo is more for calls, messages, or emails
A prestissimo adds extra warmth – great for friends!
Buona serata and Buona giornata are polite and common anytime

💞 Cultural Insight

Italian Farewells: Always Hopeful!
Italians love connections! So they have many ways to say "goodbye" that mean "see you soon." Like A dopo! (See you later!) or Ci vediamo! (See you in person!).

Buona giornata: A Kind Wish
Saying Buona giornata! (Have a good day!) is more than just "goodbye." It's a nice wish to make someone's day better. It leaves a good impression!
