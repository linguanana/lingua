✅ Level 1 (A0): Say Hello!


🔐 Key Phrase

Ciao! – Hi / Bye! （嗨／掰）
Buongiorno! – Good morning! （早安／你好）
Buonasera! – Good evening! （晚上好）
Salve! – Hello (formal or neutral) （您好）

🎯 Choose the Right Greeting


🕗 Morning at a hotel or store?
Say Buongiorno! – polite and common before 5 p.m.

🧑‍🍳 At a clothing store or café?
Say Buongiorno! when you walk in – it’s polite and friendly!

🌆 Evening at a restaurant or event?
Say Buonasera! – evening greeting after 5–6 p.m.

😄 Talking to friends or classmates?
Say Ciao! – casual and friendly.

👩‍⚕️ Not sure what to say?
Use Salve! – polite, neutral, good for anyone.

👋 Leaving a short meeting or chat?
Say A presto! – see you soon.

🙋‍♀️ Greeting a group (e.g. in class)?
Say Ciao a tutti! – Hi everyone!

🤖 Task or homework

Go to a local café or market and say “Ciao!” or “Buongiorno!” to someone new. Notice how they respond.

📌 Tip

“Ciao” - is informal and very common in Italy. （口語用法，適合朋友之間）
“Buongiorno” - is polite and used until about 5 p.m. （較正式，通常在下午 5 點前使用）
“Buonasera” - is for after 5–6 p.m. （傍晚以後的問候語）
“Salve” - is a nice neutral option if you're unsure which to use.
You can use “Ciao” for both greeting and saying goodbye!

✅ Lesson 2: More Greetings & Goodbyes

🔐 More Phrases

Buon pomeriggio! – Good afternoon（午安）
Buonanotte! – Good night, before bed（睡前說晚安）
Arrivederci! – Goodbye (formal)（正式的再見）
A dopo! – See you later today（今天稍後見）
A presto! – See you soon（很快再見）
A domani! – See you tomorrow（明天見）
Piacere! – Nice to meet you（初次見面）
Ciao ciao! – Bye-bye!（可愛口吻的掰掰）

🧪 Task or Homework

Try using these greetings in real life! Say “Buonanotte” before bed, or “A domani” after class or work.

📌 Tip

Buonanotte is only for bedtime 🌙
Arrivederci is more formal than Ciao
A presto and A dopo are good when you’ll see someone again soon
Piacere is for first meetings

✅ Lesson 3: Even More Ways to Say Goodbye

🔐 More Casual & Online Phrases

A dopo! – See you later（待會見）
A dopo ragazzi! – See you later, guys!（大家待會見）
A più tardi! – See you later today（今天稍後見）
Ci vediamo! – See you in person（到時見）
Ci vediamo domani! – See you tomorrow（明天見面）
Ci sentiamo! – Talk soon（再聯絡）
Ci sentiamo domani! – Talk to you tomorrow（明天再聯絡）
A prestissimo! – See you very soon（非常快再見）
Buona serata! – Have a nice evening（祝你今晚愉快）
Buona giornata! – Have a good day（祝你今天愉快）
Alla prossima! – See you next time（下次見）
Alla prossima settimana! – See you next week（下週見）
Alla prossima volta! – Until next time（等下次見面）

🤖 Task or Homework

Try ending a call or message in Italian! Say “Ci sentiamo” online, or “A presto!” after a class or meeting.

📌 Tip

Ci vediamo is used when you expect to meet in person
Ci sentiamo is more for calls, messages, or emails
A prestissimo adds extra warmth – great for friends!
Buona serata and Buona giornata are polite and common anytime
