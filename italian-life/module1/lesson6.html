✅ Level 1 (A0): Basic polite words

🔐 Key Phrases

Grazie – Thank you（謝謝）
Prego – You’re welcome（不客氣）
Per favore – Please（請）
Scusa – Excuse me / Sorry (informal)（不好意思，非正式）
Scusi – Excuse me / Sorry (formal)（對陌生人或長輩）
Mi scusi – Excuse me (more polite to get attention)（更正式用於引起注意）
Di niente – No problem / Don’t mention it（沒關係）
Signore – Mr. / Sir (先生 / 紳士)
Signora – Mrs. / Madam (女士 / 太太)
Signori – Ladies and gentlemen / Sirs (先生們女士們 / 各位)

🎯 Dialogue

🩷 Dialogue 1: At the market (市場對話)

🧸 : Per favore, Signore, un chilo di mele.
→ Please, sir, one kilo of apples.（請給我一公斤蘋果，先生）

👨 : Ecco a te.
→ Here you go.（給你）

🧸 : Grazie!
→ Thank you!（謝謝）

👨 : Prego!
→ You’re welcome!（不客氣）

🧸 : Per favore, anche una banana.
→ Please, also a banana.（再加一根香蕉）

👨 : Ecco.
→ Here.（來了）

🧸 : Grazie mille!
→ Thank you very much!（非常感謝）

👨 : Prego, buona giornata!
→ You’re welcome. Have a good day!（不客氣，祝你有美好的一天）


🩷 Dialogue 2: Passing by and asking for directions (通行與問路)

🧸 : Scusi, Signora, posso passare?
→ Excuse me, madam, can I pass?（不好意思，女士，我可以過嗎？）

👩‍ : Certo, scusa!
→ Of course, sorry!（當然可以，不好意思）

🧸 : Mi scusi, Signore, dov’è il bagno?
→ Excuse me, sir, where is the bathroom?（請問，先生，廁所在哪裡？）

👨 : Lì, a sinistra.
→ There, to the left.（在那邊，左手邊）

🧸 : Grazie!
→ Thank you!（謝謝）<

👨 : Di niente!
→ No problem!（沒關係）

🧸 : Arrivederci!
→ Goodbye!（再見！）

🤖 Task

1. Try saying “Grazie” and “Prego” with a friend.
2. Ask for something using “Per favore” and respond with “Prego!”.
3. Try saying “Scusi” politely to a shop assistant or stranger.

📌 Tip

💠 Grazie mille = Thanks a lot (非常感謝)

💠 Prego can also mean

– Please, go ahead (請)

– You may enter/proceed (請便) (ex：Prego, entri!)

💠 Di niente is casual, meaning "nothing" or "No problem" (沒關係)

💠 Scusa (informal) vs. Scusi / Mi scusi (formal)

– Use Scusa with friends/family

– Use Scusi or Mi scusi with strangers or in formal settings.

– Mi scusi is very polite and used to get attention. (用來引起對方注意)


💠 When to use Prego vs. Di niente:

👧 A: Grazie per il caffè!

👦 B: Prego!

Prego :Use for giving things (food, drinks, store help). It's the standard 'you're welcome.


👧 A: Grazie per l’aiuto di oggi!

👦 B: Di niente!

Di niente :Use in casual situations to say "It's nothing" or "No problem." (較輕鬆口語)

✅ Level 2 (A0+): Daily Social Etiquette & Courtesy

🔐 Key Phrases

Piacere – Nice to meet you（很高興認識你/您）
Come sta/stai? – How are you? (formal/informal)（您/你好嗎？）
Sto bene, grazie. – I'm well, thank you.（我很好，謝謝）
Non capisco. – I don't understand.（我不懂）
Può ripetere, per favore? – Can you repeat, please? (formal)（您能再說一次嗎？請）
Mi dispiace molto. – I'm very sorry.（我非常抱歉/遺憾）
Con piacere. – With pleasure (response to an offer/request)（很樂意 / 欣然）
A dopo! – See you later!（待會見！）
Buon fine settimana! – Have a good weekend!（週末愉快！）
Mi scusi per il disturbo. – Excuse me for the trouble.（抱歉打擾了）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🩷 Scenario: Meeting someone and polite small talk (初次見面與簡短客氣寒暄)**

🧸 Laura: Buongiorno, sono Laura. Piacere!
→ Good morning, I'm Laura. Nice to meet you!（早安，我是 Laura。很高興認識您！）

👨 Marco: Piacere! Io sono Marco. Come sta?
→ Nice to meet you! I'm Marco. How are you?

🧸 Laura: Sto bene, grazie. E Lei?
→ I'm well, thank you. And you?（我很好，謝謝。您呢？）

👨 Marco: Sto bene anch'io. Mi scusi, non ho capito. Può ripetere, per favore?
→ I'm well too. Excuse me, I didn't understand. Can you repeat, please?（我也很好。不好意思，我沒聽懂。請您能再說一次嗎？）

🧸 Laura: Certo. A presto!
→ Certainly. See you soon!（當然。再見！）

👨 Marco: A presto! Buon fine settimana!
→ See you soon! Have a good weekend!（再見！週末愉快！）

🤖 Task or Homework

1. Practice introducing yourself and someone else using “Piacere” and asking “Come sta/stai?”. 2. If you don't understand something, practice saying “Non capisco. Può ripetere, per favore?”. 3. Practice using different polite farewells like “A dopo!” and “Buon fine settimana!”.

📌 Tip

💠 **Expanding your social vocabulary:**

- Piacere is a common and polite way to acknowledge an introduction. (「很高興認識你」是常見且禮貌的介紹回應方式)

- Asking Come sta? (formal) or Come stai? (informal) is a standard polite greeting after initial introductions. (問候「您好嗎？」或「你好嗎？」是初次見面後的標準客氣問候語)

- Using Può ripetere, per favore? is a very polite way to ask for clarification, showing respect. (使用「您能再說一次嗎？請」是非常禮貌的請求澄清方式，表示尊重)

- Con piacere is a warm and eager response to a request or offer, showing willingness. (「很樂意」是對請求或提議的熱情回應，表示樂意幫忙)

- Apologies like Mi dispiace molto are for more significant regrets compared to the lighter Scusa/Scusi for minor inconveniences. (「我非常抱歉」用於表達更深的歉意，而「不好意思」則用於輕微的不便。)

✅ Level 3 (A1): Polite Requests & Offers

🔐 Key Phrases

Posso...? – Can I...? / May I...? (我可以...嗎？用於請求或許可)
Vorrei... – I would like... (我想要.../我希望...，比 "voglio" 更客氣)
C'è...? / Ci sono...? – Is there...? / Are there...? (有...嗎？用於詢問存在或地點)
Le/Ti posso aiutare? – Can I help you? (我能幫助您/你嗎？正式/非正式)
Certamente / Certo – Certainly / Of course (當然可以)
Volentieri – Gladly / With pleasure (樂意)
Mi dispiace – I'm sorry / I regret (我很抱歉/遺憾，比 "scusa" 更嚴肅的道歉)
Con permesso – Excuse me (for passing or interrupting politely)（借過 / 恕我打擾，用於需要通過或打斷時）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧑‍🏫 **Scenario: Asking for information in a shop (在商店詢問資訊)**

🧸 Learner: Mi scusi, Signora, posso chiederle un'informazione?
→ Excuse me, Madam, may I ask you for some information?（不好意思，女士，我可以向您詢問一些資訊嗎？）

👩‍🦱 Shopkeeper: Certamente, prego!
→ Certainly, please go ahead!（當然可以，請說吧！）

🧸 Learner: Vorrei comprare un souvenir. C'è un negozio di souvenir qui vicino?
→ I would like to buy a souvenir. Is there a souvenir shop nearby?（我想要買紀念品。附近有紀念品店嗎？）

👩‍🦱 Shopkeeper: Sì, c'è un negozio lì, a destra.
→ Yes, there's a shop there, to the right.（有，那邊右手邊有一家店。）

🧸 Learner: Grazie mille!
→ Thank you very much!（非常感謝！）

👩‍🦱 Shopkeeper: Prego!
→ You're welcome!（不客氣！）

🤖 Task or Homework

1. Practice asking for permission using “Posso...?” in different situations (e.g., to open a window, to sit here). 2. Imagine you are in a shop and use “Vorrei...” to ask for three different items politely. 3. Role-play offering help to someone using “Le/Ti posso aiutare?” and practice accepting (`Sì, grazie!`) or declining (`No, grazie!`).

📌 Tip

💠 **Expanding your politeness:**

- Posso...? is a versatile way to ask for permission or make a polite request. (「我可以...嗎？」是個多用途的禮貌請求方式)

- Vorrei... is much more polite than Voglio... ("I want...") when expressing desires. (「我想要...」比「我想要...」更客氣)

- C'è...? / Ci sono...? is useful for asking if something exists or is available. (「有...嗎？」用於詢問某物是否存在或是否可用)

- Mi dispiace is a stronger apology than Scusa/Scusi, implying regret. (「我很抱歉」比「不好意思」更強調遺憾)

- Remember to choose between informal (Ti posso aiutare?) and formal (Le posso aiutare?) forms based on your relationship with the person. (根據對象關係選擇非正式或正式的表達方式)
