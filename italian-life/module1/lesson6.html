🇮🇹 Module 1 – Lesson 6: Parole di cortesia (感謝與禮貌詞)

🎬 Theme: Learn to say “thank you,” “please,” and more in daily interactions
📚 Lessons: 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7

✅ Level 1 (A0): Basic polite words

🔐 Key Phrases

Grazie – Thank you（謝謝）
Prego – You’re welcome（不客氣）
Per favore – Please（請）
Scusa – Excuse me / Sorry（不好意思，非正式）
Scusi – Excuse me / Sorry（對陌生人）
Mi scusi – Excuse me (more polite)（更正式的不好意思）
Di niente – No problem / Don’t mention it（沒關係）

🎯 Dialogue

Dialogue 1:

🧸 Bimba: Per favore, un chilo di mele.
→ Please, one kilo of apples.（請給我一公斤蘋果）

👨 Venditore: Ecco a te.
→ Here you go.（給你）

🧸 Bimba: Grazie!
→ Thank you!（謝謝）

👨 Venditore: Prego!
→ You’re welcome!（不客氣）

🧸 Bimba: Per favore, anche una banana.
→ Please, also a banana.（再加一根香蕉）

👨 Venditore: Ecco.
→ Here.（來了）

🧸 Bimba: Grazie mille!
→ Thank you very much!（非常感謝）

👨 Venditore: Prego, buona giornata!
→ You’re welcome. Have a good day!（不客氣，祝你有美好的一天）
Dialogue 2:

🧸 Bimba: Scusa, posso passare?
→ Excuse me, can I pass?（不好意思，我可以過嗎？）

👩‍🦱 Signora: Certo, scusa!
→ Of course, sorry!（當然可以，不好意思）

🧸 Bimba: Mi scusi, dov’è il bagno?
→ Excuse me, where is the bathroom?（請問，廁所在哪裡？）

👨 Venditore: Lì, a sinistra.
→ There, to the left.（在那邊，左手邊）

🧸 Bimba: Grazie!
→ Thank you!（謝謝）

👨 Venditore: Di niente!
→ No problem!（沒關係）

🧸 Bimba: Arrivederci!
→ Goodbye!（再見！）

🌟 Task

1. Practice saying “Grazie” and “Prego” with a friend
2. Ask something using “Per favore” and respond with “Prego!”
3. Try saying “Scusi” politely in a shop setting

📌 Tip

✅ “Grazie mille” means “Thanks a lot”
✅ Use “Scusa” with friends, “Scusi” with strangers
✅ “Prego” can also mean “Please, go ahead” (e.g., Prego, entri!)
✅ “Di niente” is common and casual
✅ “Mi scusi” is very polite – use it to get attention

👧 A: Grazie per il caffè!
👦 B: Prego!
✔️ Commonly used when someone gives or serves you something — like food, drinks, or help at a store.
（常見於別人給你東西、點餐或購物時）

👧 A: Grazie per l’aiuto di oggi!
👦 B: Di niente!
✔️ Used in friendly situations to say "It’s nothing" or "No problem" — more casual and informal than "Prego".
（較輕鬆口語，用於熟人之間，比 "Prego" 更隨性）
