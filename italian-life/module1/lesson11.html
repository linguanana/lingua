✅ Level 1 (A0): Say Hello!

🎯 Learning Objectives

In this lesson, you will learn to:
  • Use **basic Italian greetings** like "Ciao," "Buongiorno," "Buonasera," and "Salve."
  • Understand the **informal and formal contexts** for common greetings.
  • Practice **pronouncing** essential Italian sounds.

🔐 Key Phrases & Usage

**1. Ciao!** – Hi / Bye! （嗨／掰）

* **Ciao!** (Hi!) * **Ciao, Mario!** (Hi, Mario!) * **Ciao a tutti!** (Hello everyone!)

📌 **Cultural Note:** "Ciao" is very common and versatile! It's used for **both "hello" and "goodbye"** but only in **informal situations** (with friends, family, or people you know well). Think of it like "Hi" or "Bye-bye" in English.

**2. Buongiorno!** – Good morning! （早安／你好）

* **Buongiorno!** (Good morning!) * **Buongiorno, signora!** (Good morning, madam!) * **Buongiorno, professore.** (Good morning, professor.)

📌 **Cultural Note:** "Buongiorno" is a **polite and formal** greeting used throughout the morning and generally until **late afternoon** (around 5 PM). It's a safe choice when you're unsure if "Ciao" is appropriate.

**3. Buonasera!** – Good evening! （晚上好）

* **Buonasera!** (Good evening!) * **Buonasera, dottore.** (Good evening, doctor.) * **Buonasera a tutti.** (Good evening, everyone.)

📌 **Cultural Note:** "Buonasera" is used for greetings **after 5-6 PM**, when it starts to get dark or the workday ends. Like "Buongiorno," it's a polite, formal option.

**4. Salve!** – Hello (formal or neutral) （您好）

* **Salve!** (Hello!) * **Salve, come sta?** (Hello, how are you? - formal)

📌 **Cultural Note:** "Salve" is a great **neutral option**. It's polite but less formal than "Buongiorno" or "Buonasera," making it a good choice if you're unsure about the appropriate level of formality.

📝 Grammar Bites: "Sono" (I am)

Click to learn about "Sono"

In Italian, **"Sono"** means **"I am"**.

It comes from the verb **"essere"** (to be), which is one of the most important verbs in Italian!

You can use it to introduce yourself:

  • **Sono [Your Name].** (I am [Your Name].)
  • **Sono Maria.** (I am Maria.)

We'll learn more about "essere" later, but for now, remember "Sono" means "I am."

🎯 Dialogue


👩 A: Ciao!
→ Hi! （嗨！）

👨 B: Buongiorno!
→ Good morning! （你好！）

👩 A: Salve!
→ Hello (formal) （您好）

👨 B: Buonasera!
→ Good evening! （晚上好）

👩 A: Ciao, a presto!
→ Bye, see you soon! （掰，回頭見！）
