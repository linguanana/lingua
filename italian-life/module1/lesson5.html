✅ Level 1 – A0: Subject pronouns


📗 Key Vocabulary

English Chinese Italian
I我io
you (informal)你tu
he他lui
she她lei
we我們noi
you (plural)你們voi
they他們 / 她們loro

📌 Tip

Italian subject pronouns are usually dropped — verbs show who’s speaking.
But for emphasis or clarity, they can be used (e.g., Io sono di Roma).
“Tu” is used informally. Use “Lei” (capitalized) for formal you.

✅ Level 2 – A0+: Possessive adjectives


📗 Key Vocabulary

English Chinese Italian
my我的mio / mia
your你的tuo / tua
his他的suo
her她的sua

📌 Tip

Possessives agree with the gender of the thing possessed, not the owner.
e.g., la mia casa (my house), il mio libro (my book).
Use definite article (il, la) with possessives most of the time.
