EN:
Let’s look at more essential Italian question words.
We’ll go through them one by one — with examples.
{pause=1000}

First,
{pause=500}Quando{pause=500} — it means “when.”
You use it to ask about time.
For example:
{pause=500}Quando parte il treno?{pause=500}
When does the train leave?
{pause=1000}

Next,
{pause=500}Perché{pause=500} — this means “why.”
Use it when you want to know the reason for something.
For example:
{pause=500}Perché sei triste?{pause=500}
Why are you sad?
{pause=1000}

Now let’s look at:
{pause=1000}Quale{pause=500} — which.
This word is useful when you’re choosing between options.
For example:
{pause=500}Quale gelato vuoi?{pause=500}
Which ice cream do you want?
{pause=1000}

Now let’s talk about “how much.”
There are two forms, depending on gender.

{pause=1000}Quanto{pause=500} — masculine singular.
Use it for masculine and uncountable nouns.
Example:
{pause=500}Quanto costa?{pause=500}
How much does it cost?
{pause=1000}

{pause=1000}Quanta{pause=500} — feminine singular.
Use it with feminine uncountable nouns.
Example:
{pause=500}Quanta acqua bevi?{pause=500}
How much water do you drink?
{pause=1000}

Now let’s talk about “how many.”
These are used when the noun is countable — and plural.

{pause=1000}Quanti{pause=500} — masculine plural.
For example:
{pause=500}Quanti amici hai?{pause=500}
How many friends do you have?
{pause=1000}

{pause=1000}Quante{pause=500} — feminine plural.
For example:
{pause=500}Quante mele vuoi?{pause=500}
How many apples do you want?
{pause=1000}

You’re doing great!
