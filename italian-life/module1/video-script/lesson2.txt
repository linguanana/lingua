Let’s look at different ways to ask and answer {{pause=500}}How are you?{{pause=500}} in Italian.

{{pause=1000}}Come stai?{{pause=1000}}
It means “How are you?” — casually. {{pause=500}}
Use it with friends or people your age. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}Come sta?{{pause=1000}}
Also means “How are you?” — but politely. {{pause=500}}
Use it with strangers, teachers, or someone older. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}Come va?{{pause=1000}}
It means “How’s it going?” {{pause=500}}
This one is relaxed and friendly. {{pause=500}}
Great for work, casual chats, or daily encounters {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}Tutto bene?{{pause=1000}}
It means “Everything good?” — quick and cheerful. {{pause=500}}
Say it when you bump into someone, like in the hallway. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=1000}}Va tutto bene?{{pause=1000}}
It means “Is everything going well?” {{pause=500}}
Use this if someone looks worried or tired. {{pause=500}}
For example, your teacher might gently ask, {{pause=500}}“Va tutto bene?”{{pause=500}}
 if you look down or seem distracted. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}Stai bene?{{pause=1000}}
It means “Are you okay?” {{pause=500}}
Use this when someone seems sick or upset. {{pause=500}}
It shows you care. {{pause=1000}}

Let’s go over how to answer “How are you?” in Italian. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=1000}}Bene{{pause=1000}} means “I’m good.”{{pause=500}}
This is a short, friendly, and very common way to say you're good {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}Sto bene{{pause=1000}} means “I’m fine.{{pause=500}}
This is a complete, direct, and widely used answer. {{pause=500}}
It's always a safe and natural response.{{pause=500}}


{{pause=1000}}Tutto bene{{pause=1000}} or {{pause=1000}}Va tutto bene{{pause=1000}} means “Everything’s good” or “Everything’s going well.”
It sounds friendly, calm, and often used in casual conversation. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=1000}}Molto bene{{pause=1000}} means “very good.”
you Use this when you're feeling particularly good or things are going exceptionally well. {{pause=500}}


{{pause=1000}}Sto benissimo{{pause=1000}} means “I’m great!”{{pause=300}}
Use it when you’re feeling fantastic. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=1000}}Così così{{pause=1000}} means “so-so.”{{pause=300}}
Not great, not terrible — just okay. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=1000}}Non c'è male{{pause=1000}} means "not bad"{{pause=300}}
A common, slightly understated way to say things are alright.

{{pause=1000}}Non molto bene{{pause=1000}} means “not very well.”{{pause=300}}
Say this if you’re feeling down, but want to be polite. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=1000}}Sono stanco{{pause=1000}} is for males or {{pause=1000}}Sono stanca{{pause=1000}} for female, means “I’m tired.”
A common and honest answer. {{pause=1000}}

And finally — {{pause=1000}}Sto bene, grazie! E tu? {{pause=1000}}
This is a full, polite response that also remembers to ask the other person how they are doing
{{pause=500}}always a good habit!{{pause=1000}}
