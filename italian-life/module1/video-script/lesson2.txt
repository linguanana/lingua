Let’s look at different ways to ask and answer {{pause=2000}}“How are you?”{{pause=2000}} in Italian.

{{pause=2000}}“Come stai?”{{pause=2000}}
is casual — for friends or people your age. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}“Come sta?”{{pause=2000}}
is the polite version — use it in formal situations. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}“Come va?”{{pause=2000}}
is friendly and can be used almost anytime. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}“Tutto bene?”{{pause=2000}}
means “Everything good?” — it’s often used if someone looks a bit tired or upset,
or just to gently check in. {{pause=1000}}

If someone asks how you are, you can say…
{{pause=2000}}“Bene”{{pause=2000}} — I’m good. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=2000}}“Molto bene”{{pause=2000}} — Very good. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=2000}}“Sto bene”{{pause=2000}} — I’m fine. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=2000}}“Sto benissimo!”{{pause=2000}} — I’m great! {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=2000}}“Così così”{{pause=2000}} — So-so. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=2000}}“Non molto bene”{{pause=2000}} — Not very well. {{pause=1000}}
If you’re tired, say
{{pause=2000}}“Sono stanco”{{pause=500}} or
{{pause=2000}}“Sono stanca”{{pause=2000}} if you’re a woman. {{pause=1000}}
And to ask back, just say:
{{pause=2000}}“E tu?”{{pause=2000}} — And you? {{pause=1000}}
