Let’s look at different ways to ask and answer {{pause=2000}}“How are you?”{{pause=2000}} in Italian.

{{pause=2000}}“Come stai?”{{pause=2000}}
is casual — for friends or people your age. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}“Come sta?”{{pause=2000}}
is the polite version — use it in formal situations. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}“Come va?”{{pause=2000}}
is friendly and can be used almost anytime. {{pause=1000}}

If someone asks you how you are, you can say
{{pause=2000}}“Bene”{{pause=2000}} — I’m good. {{pause=1000}}
Or
{{pause=2000}}“Così così”{{pause=2000}}
which means “so-so.” {{pause=1000}}

TEXT:
Lesson – Part 2: How to Answer "How are you?" in Italian

EN:
Let’s go a bit further with common responses. {{pause=1000}}

You can say
{{pause=2000}}"Sto bene"{{pause=2000}} — I’m fine. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}"Tutto bene?"{{pause=2000}}
means {{pause=2000}}“Everything okay?”{{pause=2000}} — good for checking in. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}"Come va?"{{pause=2000}}
you’ve seen already — it works both ways. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}“Stai bene?”{{pause=2000}}
is more direct — used when someone looks unwell. {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=2000}}"Grazie"{{pause=2000}}
means thank you — and
{{pause=2000}}“Grazie mille!”{{pause=2000}} means thank you very much. {{pause=1000}}

If you’re feeling fantastic, say
{{pause=2000}}“Sto benissimo!”{{pause=2000}}

Still just okay? You can repeat
{{pause=2000}}“Così così.”{{pause=2000}}

Not doing well?
Try {{pause=2000}}“Non molto bene.”{{pause=2000}} {{pause=1000}}

If you’re tired,
say {{pause=2000}}“Sono stanco”{{pause=2000}} — or
{{pause=2000}}“stanca”{{pause=2000}} if you’re a woman. {{pause=1000}}

And to ask the other person, simply say:
{{pause=2000}}“E tu?”{{pause=2000}} — And you? {{pause=1000}}
