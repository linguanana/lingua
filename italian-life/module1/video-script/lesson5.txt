 EN:
 {{pause=2000}}“Di dove sei?”{{pause=2000}}
 means “Where are you from?” — it’s used informally. {{pause=1000}}

 {{pause=2000}}“Di dov’è?”{{pause=2000}}
 is the formal version — use it with someone you don’t know well. {{pause=1000}}

 You can say:
 {{pause=2000}}“Io sono di Milano”{{pause=2000}} — I’m from Milan.
 Use **"Sono di..."** when you’re talking about a city. {{pause=1000}}

 Or say:
 {{pause=2000}}“Vengo da Taiwan”{{pause=2000}} — I come from Taiwan.
 Use **"Vengo da..."** for countries or to emphasize where you come from. {{pause=1000}}

 Examples:
 {{pause=2000}}“Vengo da Taiwan”{{pause=2000}} — I come from Taiwan.
 {{pause=2000}}“Vengo dagli Stati Uniti”{{pause=2000}} — I come from the United States. {{pause=1000}}

 To ask if someone is Italian:
 {{pause=2000}}“Sei italiano?” {{pause=500}} or {{pause=500}} “italiana?”{{pause=2000}}
 depending on gender. {{pause=1000}}

 If not, you might say:
 {{pause=2000}}“No, sono americano”{{pause=2000}} — I’m American.
 Or {{pause=2000}}“americana”{{pause=2000}} if you’re a woman.

EN:
To ask someone where they live, say:
{{pause=2000}}“Dove abiti?”{{pause=2000}} — informal.
Or
{{pause=2000}}“Dove vive?”{{pause=2000}} — the formal version. {{pause=1000}}

If you live in a city, say:
{{pause=2000}}“Abito a Roma”{{pause=2000}} — I live in Rome.
And to respond with “me too,” say:
{{pause=2000}}“Anch’io abito lì”{{pause=2000}} — I live there too. {{pause=1000}}

Want to know more?
Ask:
{{pause=2000}}“In quale zona?”{{pause=2000}} — In which area?
Or
{{pause=2000}}“Da quanto tempo?”{{pause=2000}} — For how long have you lived there?
