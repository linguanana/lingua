********** EN Script (talking script) **********
 {{pause=2000}}“Di dove sei?”{{pause=2000}}
 means “Where are you from?” — it’s used informally. {{pause=1000}}

 {{pause=2000}}“Di dov’è?”{{pause=2000}}
 is the formal version — use it with someone you don’t know well. {{pause=1000}}

 You can say:
 {{pause=2000}}“Io sono di Milano”{{pause=2000}} — I’m from Milan.
 Use {{pause=1000}}Sono di...{{pause=1000}} when you’re talking about a city. {{pause=1000}}

 Or say:
 {{pause=2000}}“Vengo dagli Stati Uniti”{{pause=2000}} — I come from the United States.
 Use {{pause=1000}}Vengo da...{{pause=1000}} for countries or to emphasize where you come from. {{pause=1000}}

 Or more specifically:
 {{pause=2000}}“Vengo dalla California”{{pause=2000}} — I come from California.
 You can also say your city, region, or state. {{pause=1000}}

 To ask if someone is Italian:
 {{pause=2000}}“Sei italiano?” {{pause=500}} or {{pause=500}} “italiana?”{{pause=2000}}
 depending on gender. {{pause=1000}}

 If not, you might say:
 {{pause=2000}}“No, sono americano”{{pause=2000}} — I’m American.
 Or {{pause=2000}}“americana”{{pause=2000}} if you’re a woman.

********** EN Script (talking script) **********
To ask someone where they live, say:
{{pause=2000}}“Dove abiti?”{{pause=2000}} — informal.
Or:
{{pause=2000}}“Dove vive?”{{pause=2000}} — the formal version. {{pause=1000}}

These questions are useful when you're getting to know someone.
They sound polite, but still friendly. {{pause=1000}}

If you live in a city, you can say:
{{pause=2000}}“Abito a Roma”{{pause=2000}} — I live in Rome.
Use {{pause=1000}}“Abito a…”{{pause=1000}} for cities and small towns. {{pause=1000}}

And if someone says the same place as you, you might reply:
{{pause=2000}}“Anch’io abito lì”{{pause=2000}} — I live there too! {{pause=1000}}

Want to keep the conversation going? Try asking:
{{pause=2000}}“In quale zona?”{{pause=2000}} — In which area?
or
{{pause=2000}}“Da quanto tempo?”{{pause=2000}} — For how long have you lived there? {{pause=1000}}

These are great phrases for small talk,  especially if you’re in a café or meeting someone new. {{pause=1000}}
