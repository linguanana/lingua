There are a few ways to ask and answer "What are you doing?" in Italian. {{pause=1000}}

The most neutral way to ask is  {{pause=1000}}   “Che cosa fai?” {{pause=500}}

You'll often hear a shorter, more casual version:   {{pause=1000}}  Cosa fai?” {{pause=1000}}

Now, for how to answer. {{pause=1000}}

Sometimes, Italian uses the simple present tense to talk about what you're doing right now. {{pause=1000}}

For example: {{pause=500}}

{{pause=500}}  Studio {{pause=500}}  means “I’m studying.”.” {{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}  Lavoro {{pause=500}}  means “I’m working.” {{pause=1000}}

For other actions currently in progress, you can use a more specific structure.” {{pause=1000}}
 {{pause=500}}   sto  {{pause=500}}plus the gerund form of the verb. {{pause=2000}}

Like this: {{pause=500}}
{{pause=500}}   “Sto studiando l’italiano  {{pause=500}}   means “I’m studying Italian.” {{pause=1000}}

 {{pause=500}}  Sto navigando in internet  {{pause=500}}   means “I’m surfing the internet.” {{pause=1500}}

Now, for talking about meals, it’s a bit different. {{pause=1000}}

You use the verb  {{pause=500}}fare {{pause=500}} in the simple present tense. {{pause=1000}}

 {{pause=500}}  Faccio colazione” {{pause=500}}  means “I’m having breakfast.” {{pause=800}}

 {{pause=500}}  Faccio pranzo  {{pause=500}}   means “I’m having lunch.” {{pause=800}}

And  {{pause=500}}  Faccio cena  {{pause=500}}  means “I’m having dinner.” {{pause=1500}}

While you might sometimes hear  {{pause=500}}  sto facendo colazione, {{pause=500}}   the simple present version,  {{pause=500}}  faccio colazione, {{pause=500}}   is more common. {{pause=1000}}

Finally, if you're not doing anything special, you can simply say: {{pause=1000}}

” {{pause=500}}  Niente {{pause=500}} or  {{pause=500}}Niente di speciale {{pause=500}}

This means, “Nothing special.” {{pause=1000}}

********** EN Script (talking script) **********
Let's learn how to talk about your job in Italian. {{pause=1000}}

To ask someone what they do for a living, you can say: {{pause=1000}}

 {{pause=500}}Che lavoro fai?”  {{pause=500}}
This is the informal way to ask. {{pause=1000}}

For a polite or formal situation, like with a stranger, you would say: {{pause=1000}}
 {{pause=500}}Che lavoro fa?”  {{pause=500}}

Now, let's look at how to answer. {{pause=1000}}

The most common way to answer is with "faccio" plus the correct article and your profession. {{pause=1500}}

For example: {{pause=500}}
 {{pause=500}}Faccio l’insegnante. {{pause=500}} which means “I’m a teacher.” {{pause=1000}}
 {{pause=500}}Faccio il medico. {{pause=500}}which means “I’m a doctor.” {{pause=1000}}

If you are a student, the words change based on gender:  {{pause=500}}
{{pause=500}}Sono studente.{{pause=500}} for a male student. {{pause=500}}
{{pause=500}}Sono studentessa.{{pause=500}} for a female student. {{pause=1000}}

You can also use  {{pause=500}}sono {{pause=500}} plus your profession. This is a bit simpler. {{pause=1500}}
 {{pause=500}}Sono insegnante.” {{pause=800}}
 {{pause=500}}Sono medico.” {{pause=1000}}

If you want to talk about where you work, you can say: {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=500}}Lavoro in un ufficio.{{pause=500}} meaning “I work in an office.” {{pause=1000}}


If you’re currently between jobs, you would say: {{pause=1500}}
{{pause=500}}Sono disoccupato.{{pause=500}} if you are male. {{pause=1000}}
{{pause=500}}Sono disoccupata.{{pause=500}} if you are female. {{pause=1-00}}
