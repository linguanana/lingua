✅ Level 1 (A0): Basic polite words

📗 Key Phrases

Grazie – Thank you（謝謝）
Prego – You’re welcome（不客氣）
Per favore – Please（請）
Scusa – Excuse me / Sorry (informal)（不好意思，非正式）
Scusi – Excuse me / Sorry (formal)（對陌生人或長輩）
Mi scusi – Excuse me (more polite to get attention)（更正式用於引起注意）
Di niente – No problem / Don’t mention it（沒關係）
Signore – Mr. / Sir (先生 / 紳士)
Signora – Mrs. / Madam (女士 / 太太)
Signori – Ladies and gentlemen / Sirs (先生們女士們 / 各位)

🎯 Dialogue

🩷 Dialogue 1: At the market (市場對話)

🧸 : Per favore, Signore, un chilo di mele.
→ Please, sir, one kilo of apples.（請給我一公斤蘋果，先生）

👨 : Ecco a te.
→ Here you go.（給你）

🧸 : Grazie!
→ Thank you!（謝謝）

👨 : Prego!
→ You’re welcome!（不客氣）

🧸 : Per favore, anche una banana.
→ Please, also a banana.（再加一根香蕉）

👨 : Ecco.
→ Here.（來了）

🧸 : Grazie mille!
→ Thank you very much!（非常感謝）

👨 : Prego, buona giornata!
→ You’re welcome. Have a good day!（不客氣，祝你有美好的一天）


🩷 Dialogue 2: Passing by and asking for directions (通行與問路)

🧸 : Scusi, Signora, posso passare?
→ Excuse me, madam, can I pass?（不好意思，女士，我可以過嗎？）

👩‍ : Certo, scusa!
→ Of course, sorry!（當然可以，不好意思）

🧸 : Mi scusi, Signore, dov’è il bagno?
→ Excuse me, sir, where is the bathroom?（請問，先生，廁所在哪裡？）

👨 : Lì, a sinistra.
→ There, to the left.（在那邊，左手邊）

🧸 : Grazie!
→ Thank you!（謝謝）<

👨 : Di niente!
→ No problem!（沒關係）

🧸 : Arrivederci!
→ Goodbye!（再見！）

🤖 Task

1. Try saying “Grazie” and “Prego” with a friend.
2. Ask for something using “Per favore” and respond with “Prego!”.
3. Try saying “Scusi” politely to a shop assistant or stranger.

📌 Tip

💠 Grazie mille = Thanks a lot (非常感謝)

💠 Prego can also mean

– Please, go ahead (請)

– You may enter/proceed (請便) (ex：Prego, entri!)

💠 Di niente is casual, meaning "nothing" or "No problem" (沒關係)

💠 Scusa (informal) vs. Scusi / Mi scusi (formal)

– Use Scusa with friends/family

– Use Scusi or Mi scusi with strangers or in formal settings.

– Mi scusi is very polite and used to get attention. (用來引起對方注意)


💠 When to use Prego vs. Di niente:

👧 A: Grazie per il caffè!

👦 B: Prego!

Prego :Use for giving things (food, drinks, store help). It's the standard 'you're welcome.


👧 A: Grazie per l’aiuto di oggi!

👦 B: Di niente!

Di niente :Use in casual situations to say "It's nothing" or "No problem." (較輕鬆口語)

Prego – You’re welcome / Go ahead / After you（不客氣 / 請便 / 您先）
Per favore – Please（請）
Scusa – Excuse me / Sorry (informal)（不好意思 / 對不起，非正式）
Scusi – Excuse me / Sorry (formal)（不好意思 / 對不起，正式）
Mi scusi – Excuse me (very polite)（對不起，打擾一下）
Di niente – No problem / Don’t mention it（沒關係 / 不客氣）
Signore / Signora – Sir / Ma’am（先生 / 女士）

✅ Level 3 (A1): Polite Requests & Offers

📗 Key Phrases

Posso...? – Can I...? / May I...?（我可以…嗎？）
Vorrei... – I would like...（比 voglio 更客氣）
C'è...? / Ci sono...? – Is there...? / Are there...?（有…嗎？）
Le / Ti posso aiutare? – Can I help you?（正式 / 非正式）
Certamente / Certo – Certainly / Of course
Volentieri – Gladly / With pleasure
Mi dispiace – I'm sorry / I regret（比 scusa 更正式）
Con permesso – Excuse me（借過 / 禮貌打斷）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🩷 Scenario: Asking for information in a shop (在商店詢問資訊)**

🧸 : Mi scusi, Signora, posso chiederle un'informazione?
→ Excuse me, Madam, may I ask you for some information?（不好意思，女士，我可以向您詢問一些資訊嗎？）

👩‍🦱 : Certamente, prego!
→ Certainly, please go ahead!（當然可以，請說吧！）

🧸 : Vorrei comprare un souvenir. C'è un negozio di souvenir qui vicino?
→ I would like to buy a souvenir. Is there a souvenir shop nearby?（我想要買紀念品。附近有紀念品店嗎？）

👩‍🦱 : Sì, c'è un negozio lì, a destra.
→ Yes, there's a shop there, to the right.（有，那邊右手邊有一家店。）

🧸 : Grazie mille!
→ Thank you very much!（非常感謝！）

👩‍🦱 : Prego!
→ You're welcome!（不客氣！）

🤖 Task or Homework

1. Use Posso...? to ask permission (e.g., open a window, sit here)
2. Use Vorrei... to politely ask for 3 things in a shop
3. Practice saying Ti/Le posso aiutare? and answer with Sì, grazie! or No, grazie!

📌 Tip

💠 Polite Phrases – Be kind and respectful

- Posso...? – May I...?（禮貌請求）

- Vorrei... is more polite than Voglio...（「我想要...」更客氣）

- C'è...? / Ci sono...? – Is there...?（詢問是否有）

- Mi dispiace shows deeper regret than Scusa/Scusi（更強烈的道歉）

- Use Ti (informal) or Le (formal) based on the person（根據對象選擇你/您）
