✅ Level 1 (A0): Ask "How are you?"

🔐 Key Phrase

Come stai? – How are you?（你好嗎？）
Come sta? – How are you? formal（您好嗎？）
Come va? – How’s it going?（最近如何？）
Bene – I’m good / Fine（我很好）
Così così – So-so（普普通通）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

👋 Greeting a friend casually
Come stai? – informal, friendly.

🧑‍🏫 Talking to a teacher or someone older
Come sta? – formal version.

🤝 Meeting someone at a café
Come va? – neutral and common.

😊 Feeling good
Bene!

😐 Feeling okay
Così così – just so-so.

😟 Not feeling great
Non molto bene – not so good.

🤖 Task or Homework

Ask someone today: “Come stai?” and answer with “Bene” or “Così così.”

📌 Tip

Try asking someone: Come stai? – “How are you?”
When replying, say: Sto bene. – “I’m fine.”
➕ Note: Sto comes from stare (to be / to stay), and bene means “well.”
💬 You can also reply simply with Beneor Così così.

✅ Level 2 (A0+): Answer with “Sto bene”

🔐 Key Phrase (Review)

Sto bene. – I’m fine.（我很好）
Tutto bene? – Everything okay?（都好嗎？）
Come va? – How’s it going?（近期如何？）
Stai bene? – Are you okay?（你還好嗎？）

🎯 Dialogue Examples

👩‍🏫 Come stai?
🧑 Sto bene, grazie! – I'm fine, thank you!

👨‍🍳 Tutto bene?
👩 Sì, sto bene! – Yes, I’m fine!

👧 Come va oggi?
👦 Sto bene. Un po’ stanco, ma bene. – I’m fine. A bit tired, but fine.

🧑‍💻 Stai bene?
👩‍🎓 Sì, sto bene adesso. – Yes, I’m okay now.

🤖 Task or Homework

Use Sto bene in a real conversation.
oggi – “today” in Italian.
adesso – “now / at this moment.”

✅ Level 3 (A1): More Emotional Replies

🎯 Phrases for How You Feel

🧑 Sto benissimo! – I’m great!（我非常好）

🧑 Così così. – So-so（馬馬虎虎）

🧑 Non molto bene. – Not very well（不太好）

🧑 Sono stanco / stanca. – I’m tired（我累了）

🧑 E tu? – And you?（你呢？）

📌 Tip

💠 Use Sto benissimo when you're feeling great

- Use “benissimo” after something great — like good news or a perfect day.

- (非常好、超棒的心情時使用，例如剛完成重要任務）

💠 Use Così così if you feel just okay

💠 Sono stanco – I’m tired (male speaker)

💠 Sono stanca – I’m tired (female speaker)
