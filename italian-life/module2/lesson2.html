✅ Level 1 (A0): Basic Pastries & Drinks

🔐 Key Phrases

Una pasta – A pastry (一個點心/糕點)
Un cornetto – A croissant (一個可頌)
Un succo di frutta – A fruit juice (一杯果汁)
Un'acqua naturale – A still water (一杯礦泉水/常溫水)
Un'acqua frizzante – A sparkling water (一杯氣泡水)

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧑‍🏫 When: You want to order a pastry. (想點糕點)
Say: Vorrei una pasta, per favore. – "I'd like a pastry, please." (我想要一個糕點)

🧑‍🏫 When: You specifically want a croissant. (想點可頌)
Say: Un cornetto, per favore. – "A croissant, please." (請來一個可頌)

🧑‍🏫 When: You want fruit juice. (想點果汁)
Say: Un succo di frutta, per favore. – "A fruit juice, please." (請來一杯果汁)

🤖 Task or Homework

Imagine ordering a `cornetto` and a `cappuccino` in Italian.

📌 Tip

💠 Italian pastries are often less sweet than American ones. (義式點心通常甜度較低)

- `Cornetto` is a common croissant-like pastry, especially in central/southern Italy. (Cornetto是義大利常見的可頌類點心)

💠 Most Italians have a quick breakfast at the `Bar`, often standing. (多數義大利人習慣在Bar快速早餐，常站著吃)

✅ Level 2 (A0+): Specifying Pastries & Hot Drinks

🔐 Key Phrases

Cornetto alla crema – Croissant with cream filling (奶油內餡可頌)
Cornetto al cioccolato – Croissant with chocolate filling (巧克力內餡可頌)
Un tè – A tea (一杯茶)
Un caffè macchiato caldo/freddo – A hot/cold macchiato (一杯熱/冰瑪奇朵)

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧑‍🏫 When: You want a specific type of croissant. (想點特定口味可頌)
Say: Vorrei un cornetto al cioccolato. – "I'd like a chocolate croissant." (我想要一個巧克力可頌)

🧑‍🏫 When: You prefer tea over coffee. (想喝茶而非咖啡)
Say: Un tè, per favore. – "A tea, please." (請來一杯茶)

🧑‍🏫 When: You want a macchiato and need to specify its temperature. (想點冷熱瑪奇朵)
Say: Un caffè macchiato freddo, per favore. – "A cold macchiato, please." (請來一杯冰瑪奇朵)

🤖 Task or Homework

Practice asking for **`un cornetto alla crema`** and **`un tè`**.

📌 Tip

💠 In Italy, "tea" often means black tea. (義大利的「茶」常指紅茶)

- For green tea: `tè verde`. For herbal tea: `tisana`. (綠茶：tè verde；花草茶：tisana)

💠 "Macchiato" means "stained." It's an espresso "stained" with a bit of milk. (瑪奇朵是「染色」咖啡，少量牛奶)

✅ Level 3 (A1): Asking for Options & Customs

🔐 Key Phrases

Cosa c'è di dolce? – What sweet things are there? (有什麼甜點？)
Qual è la pasta del giorno? – What's today's pastry? (今天有什麼點心？)
Un succo d'arancia spremuta – A freshly squeezed orange juice (一杯現榨柳橙汁)
Con ghiaccio? – With ice? (加冰塊嗎？)

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧑‍🏫 When: You want to see the variety of pastries available. (想看有什麼點心)
Say: Cosa c'è di dolce oggi? – "What sweet things are there today?" (今天有什麼甜點？)

🧑‍🏫 When: You want fresh juice, not from a carton. (想喝新鮮果汁)
Say: Vorrei un succo d'arancia spremuta. – "I'd like a freshly squeezed orange juice." (我想要一杯現榨柳橙汁)

🧑‍🏫 When: The barista asks if you want ice. (咖啡師問是否加冰塊)
Listen for: Con ghiaccio? – "With ice?" (加冰塊嗎？)
Respond: Sì, grazie. (Yes, thank you.) / No, grazie. (No, thank you.)

🤖 Task or Homework

Next time you see a pastry you like, try to describe it in Italian (e.g., `al cioccolato`, `alla crema`).

📌 Tip

💠 Italians usually don't put ice in their drinks unless asked. (義大利人飲料不常加冰)

- A `spremuta` is always fresh. `Succo d'arancia` might be from a carton. (Spremuta才是現榨果汁)

🍰 Bonus: Italian Desserts (義大利甜點)

Beyond pastries, Italy is famous for its delicious desserts! Here are some you might find in a `Bar` or restaurant:

Popular Italian Desserts (常見義大利甜點)

Tiramisù – "Pick Me Up" (提拉米蘇)

- Coffee-soaked ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, eggs, sugar, and cocoa powder. A classic creamy dessert.

Panna Cotta – "Cooked Cream" (義式奶酪)

- Sweetened cream thickened with gelatin, often served with berry sauce, caramel, or chocolate.

Gelato – Ice Cream (義式冰淇淋)

- Italian ice cream, known for its intense flavors and denser, creamier texture than regular ice cream.

Cannolo (plural: Cannoli) – "Little Tube" (西西里捲)

- Crispy fried pastry shell filled with sweet, creamy ricotta cheese, often with candied fruit or chocolate chips.

Profiteroles – Cream Puffs (泡芙)

- Small choux pastry balls filled with cream (often Chantilly or pastry cream) and typically covered in chocolate sauce.

Babà – Rum Baba (蘭姆巴巴)

- A small, yeast-leavened cake, typically soaked in rum syrup. Popular in Naples.

Millefoglie – "Thousand Leaves" (千層派)

- Italian version of the French "Mille-feuille." Layers of puff pastry and pastry cream, often topped with powdered sugar.
