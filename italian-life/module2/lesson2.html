✅ Level 1 (A0): Basic Pastries & Drinks

🔐 Key Phrases

Una pasta – A pastry（點心/糕點）
Un cornetto – A croissant（可頌）
Un dolce – A sweet/dessert（甜點）
Un panino – A sandwich（三明治）
Una focaccia – A flatbread（佛卡夏）
Una pizzetta – A small pizza（小披薩）
Una torta – A cake（蛋糕）
Un tramezzino – A triangle sandwich（冷三明治）
Un succo di frutta – A fruit juice（果汁）
Un’acqua naturale – A still water（礦泉水）
Un’acqua frizzante – A sparkling water（氣泡水）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧑‍🏫 Vorrei una pasta, per favore.
–> "I'd like a pastry, please." (我想要一個糕點)

🧑‍🏫 Un cornetto, per favore.
–> "A croissant, please." (請來一個可頌)

🧑‍🏫 Un succo d’arancia, per favore
–> "An orange juice, please." (請來一杯果汁)

🤖 Task or Homework

Imagine ordering a cornetto and a cappuccino in Italian.

📌 Tip

💠 Italian breakfast pastries are usually less sweet than American ones.（義式點心不會太甜）

- Cornetto: Like a croissant, common in central/southern Italy.（像可頌，中南部常見）

💠 Breakfast is often quick and eaten standing at the bar.（早餐多在 Bar 站著吃）

💠 “A / an” in Italian:

- un cornetto: masculine singular（陽性單數）

- una pasta: feminine singular（陰性單數）

- un'acqua: feminine starting with vowel（母音開頭的陰性）

💠 Un succo di... = a juice of...（⋯汁）

- d’arancia: orange 🍊

- di mela: apple 🍎

- di pesca: peach 🍑

- di frutta: mixed/fruit juice（混合果汁/泛指）

✅ Level 2 (A0+): Specifying Pastries & Hot Drinks

🔐 Key Phrases

Cornetto alla crema – Croissant with cream filling (奶油可頌)
Cornetto al cioccolato – Croissant with chocolate filling (巧克力可頌)
Un tè – A tea (茶)
Un caffè macchiato caldo/freddo – A hot/cold macchiato (熱/冰瑪奇朵)

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧡 Want to see today’s sweets?
👩‍🦰 Cosa c’è di dolce oggi?
→ What sweet things are there today?（今天有什麼甜點？）

🧡 Want freshly squeezed juice.
👩‍🦰 Vorrei un succo d’arancia spremuta.
→ I’d like a freshly squeezed orange juice.（我想要一杯現榨柳橙汁）

🧡 The barista asks if you want ice.
👩‍🦰 Con ghiaccio?
→ With ice?（加冰塊嗎？）
👨‍🍳 Sì, grazie. / No, grazie.

🧡 Ask for milk.
👩‍🦰 Posso avere un po’ di latte?
→ Can I have a little milk?（可以給我一點牛奶嗎？）

🧡 Ask for sugar.
👩‍🦰 Posso avere dello zucchero?
→ Can I have some sugar?（可以給我一些糖嗎？）

🧡 Ask for the most popular drink.
👩‍🦰 Qual è la bevanda più popolare?
→ What’s the most popular drink?（最受歡迎的飲料是什麼？）

🤖 Task or Homework

Practice asking for un cornetto alla crema and un tè.

📌 Tip

💠 In Italy, "tea" often means black tea. (義大利茶多指紅茶)

- For green tea: tè verde

- For herbal tea: tisana

💠 Macchiato “stained” in Italian, means espresso + a bit milk.

not like the sweet American macchiato! 濃縮咖啡+一點牛奶，not美式的甜瑪奇朵

🤖 Task or Homework

📌 Tip

💠 Italians don’t usually add ice unless you ask.（義大利人飲料不常加冰）

💠 Spremuta = fresh-squeezed juice
Succo d’arancia = orange juice, often from a carton

💠 Cosa c’è di dolce? = What sweet things are there?（今天有什麼甜點？）

💠 Qual è la pasta del giorno? = What’s today’s pastry?（今日甜點是什麼？）

💠 Posso avere... = Can I have...（我可以要⋯嗎？）
➤ un po’ di latte = a little milk
➤ dello zucchero = some sugar

💠 Qual è la bevanda più popolare? = What’s the most popular drink?（最受歡迎的飲料是什麼？）

🍰 Bonus: Italian Desserts (義大利甜點)

Beyond pastries, Italy is famous for its delicious desserts!
Here are some you might find in a Bar or restaurant:

Popular Italian Desserts (常見義大利甜點)

Tiramisù – "Pick Me Up" (提拉米蘇)
- Coffee ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, cocoa. Classic creamy dessert.

Panna Cotta – "Cooked Cream" (義式奶酪)
- Sweet cream with gelatin. Often with berry, caramel, or chocolate sauce.

Gelato – Ice Cream (義式冰淇淋)
- Italian ice cream. Intense flavor, dense and creamy

Cannolo (plural: Cannoli) – "Little Tube" (西西里捲)
- Crispy fried pastry shell filled with sweet ricotta cheese. Often with candied fruit or chocolate.

Profiteroles – Cream Puffs (泡芙)
- Small cream-filled pastry balls, usually covered in chocolate sauce

Babà – Rum Baba (蘭姆巴巴)
- Small cake soaked in rum syrup. Popular in Naples

Millefoglie – "Thousand Leaves" (千層派)
- Layers of puff pastry and pastry cream. Often topped with powdered sugar
