✅ Level 1 (A0): Basic Pastries & Drinks

🔐 Key Phrases

Una pasta – A pastry（點心/糕點）
Un cornetto – A croissant（可頌）
Un dolce – A sweet/dessert（甜點）
Un panino – A sandwich（三明治）
Una focaccia – A flatbread（佛卡夏）
Una pizzetta – A small pizza（小披薩）
Una torta – A cake（蛋糕）
Un tramezzino – A triangle sandwich（冷三明治）
Un succo di frutta – A fruit juice（果汁）
Un’acqua naturale – A still water（礦泉水）
Un’acqua frizzante – A sparkling water（氣泡水）

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧑‍🏫 Vorrei una pasta, per favore.
–> "I'd like a pastry, please." (我想要一個糕點)

🧑‍🏫 Un cornetto, per favore.
–> "A croissant, please." (請來一個可頌)

🧑‍🏫 Un succo d’arancia, per favore
–> "An orange juice, please." (請來一杯果汁)

🤖 Task or Homework

Imagine ordering a cornetto and a cappuccino in Italian.

📌 Tip

💠 Italian pastries are often less sweet than American ones. (義式點心通常較不甜)

- Cornetto is like a croissant, common in central/southern Italy. (Cornetto像可頌，中南義常見)

💠 Italians usually eat breakfast quickly at the Bar, often standing. (義大利人早餐常在Bar站著吃，快速解決)

💠 Understanding "a/an"

- un cornetto : masculine singular nouns 陽性單數名詞

- una pasta : feminine singular nouns 陰性單數名詞

- una acqua = un'acqua : feminine singular nouns starting with a vowel 母音開頭的陰性單數名詞

💠 Un succo = a juice | di = of

💠 Most common italian juice is succo d’arancia orange juice, others

- succo di mela apple juice- succo di pesca peach juice

- succo di frutta in gernal juice

✅ Level 2 (A0+): Specifying Pastries & Hot Drinks

🔐 Key Phrases

Cornetto alla crema – Croissant with cream filling (奶油可頌)
Cornetto al cioccolato – Croissant with chocolate filling (巧克力可頌)
Un tè – A tea (茶)
Un caffè macchiato caldo/freddo – A hot/cold macchiato (熱/冰瑪奇朵)

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

👩: Vorrei un cornetto al cioccolato.
→ I'd like a chocolate croissant.（我想要一個巧克力可頌）

🧑: Subito!
→ Right away! / Coming right up!（馬上來！）

👩: Un tè, per favore.
→ A tea, please.（請給我一杯茶）

🧑: Prego!
→ Here you go! / You're welcome!（請用！/ 不客氣）

👩: Un caffè macchiato freddo, per favore.
→ A cold macchiato, please.（請給我一杯冰的瑪奇朵）

🧑: Ecco a Lei!
→ Here you are! (formal)（給您！ – 正式）
🧑: Ecco a Te!
→ Here you are! (informal)（給你！ – 非正式）

🤖 Task or Homework

Practice asking for un cornetto alla crema and un tè.

📌 Tip

💠 In Italy, "tea" often means black tea. (義大利茶多指紅茶)

- For green tea: tè verde

- For herbal tea: tisana

💠 Macchiato “stained” in Italian, means espresso + a bit milk.

not like the sweet American macchiato! 濃縮咖啡+一點牛奶，not美式的甜瑪奇朵

✅ Level 3 (A1): Asking for Options & Customs

🔐 Key Phrases

Cosa c'è di dolce? – What sweet things are there? (有什麼甜點？)
Qual è la pasta del giorno? – What's today's pastry? (今天有什麼點心？)
Un succo d'arancia spremuta – A freshly squeezed orange juice (現榨柳橙汁)
Con ghiaccio? – With ice? (加冰塊嗎？)
Posso avere un po' di latte? – Can I have some milk? (可以給我點牛奶嗎？)
Posso avere dello zucchero? – Can I have some sugar? (可以給我點糖嗎？)
Qual è la bevanda più popolare? – What's the most popular drink? (最受歡迎的飲料是什麼？)

🎯 Dialogue / When to use

🧡 want to see the variety of pastries today.
👩: Cosa c'è di dolce oggi? – "What sweet things are there today?" (今天有什麼甜點？)

🧡 want freshly squeezed orange juice.
👩: Vorrei un succo d'arancia spremuta. – "I'd like a freshly squeezed orange juice." (我想要一杯現榨柳橙汁)

‍🧡 The barista asks if you want ice.
‍ 👩: Con ghiaccio? – "With ice?" (加冰塊嗎？)
🧑: Sì, grazie. / No, grazie.

🧡 ask for milk.
👩: Posso avere un po' di latte? – Can I have a little milk? 可以給我一點牛奶嗎

🧡 ask for sugar.
👩: Posso avere dello zucchero? – Can I have some sugar? 可以給我一些糖嗎

🧡 ask for the most popular drink.
👩: Qual è la bevanda più popolare? – "What's the most popular drink?" (最受歡迎的飲料是什麼？)

🤖 Task or Homework

Next time you see a pastry you like, try to describe it in Italian (e.g., al cioccolato, alla crema).

📌 Tip

💠 Italians usually don't put ice in their drinks unless asked. (義大利飲料不常加冰)

- Spremuta is fresh squeezed. (Spremuta是現榨)

- Succo d'arancia might be from a carton (Succo d'arancia盒裝)

💠 Cosa c'è di dolce?:

• Cosa: "What"

• c'è: "There is" (from ci + è)

• di dolce: "Of sweet" (refers to sweet items/desserts)

💠 Qual è la pasta del giorno:

• Qual è: "What/Which is"

• la pasta: "The pastry" (general term)

• del giorno: "Of the day" (di + il)

💠 Un succo d'arancia spremuta:

• spremuta: "Squeezed/pressed"

💠 Posso avere "un po' di latte" | "dello zucchero":

• Posso: Can I/May I (from potere)

• avere: to have/to get

• un po' di: A little bit of/some

• dello : some

💠 Qual è la bevanda più popolare:

• più: "More/most"

• popolare: "Popular"


🍰 Bonus: Italian Desserts (義大利甜點)

Beyond pastries, Italy is famous for its delicious desserts!
Here are some you might find in a Bar or restaurant:

Popular Italian Desserts (常見義大利甜點)

Tiramisù – "Pick Me Up" (提拉米蘇)
- Coffee ladyfingers, mascarpone cheese, cocoa. Classic creamy dessert.

Panna Cotta – "Cooked Cream" (義式奶酪)
- Sweet cream with gelatin. Often with berry, caramel, or chocolate sauce.

Gelato – Ice Cream (義式冰淇淋)
- Italian ice cream. Intense flavor, dense and creamy

Cannolo (plural: Cannoli) – "Little Tube" (西西里捲)
- Crispy fried pastry shell filled with sweet ricotta cheese. Often with candied fruit or chocolate.

Profiteroles – Cream Puffs (泡芙)
- Small cream-filled pastry balls, usually covered in chocolate sauce

Babà – Rum Baba (蘭姆巴巴)
- Small cake soaked in rum syrup. Popular in Naples

Millefoglie – "Thousand Leaves" (千層派)
- Layers of puff pastry and pastry cream. Often topped with powdered sugar
