========== 🩷🩷 5

(頁面)：
👋 General Goodbyes
Au revoir – Goodbye (standard and polite)
Salut – Bye (informal, also means "hi")

🫶 Friendly / Unspecified Reunions
À bientôt – See you soon (unspecified time)
À très vite – See you very soon! (enthusiastic)

🕒 Later Today
À tout à l’heure – See you later (today)
À plus tard – See you later today (casual/emphasized)

📅 Next Time / Scheduled
À demain – See you tomorrow
À la prochaine – Until next time
À la semaine prochaine – See you next week

👀 Keeping in Touch
On se voit – See you (in person)
On se parle – Talk to you soon (text or call)

🌞 Polite Wishes
Bonne journée ! – Have a good day
Bonne soirée ! – Have a good evening

==========🎤 English Voice (講稿)：==========
Au revoir {{pause=1000}}
A very standard way to say goodbye. {{pause=500}}
You can use it almost everywhere — shops, cafés, hotels. {{pause=1000}}

Salut ! {{pause=1000}}
Means both “hi” and “bye”! {{pause=500}}
But only use it with friends — it’s informal. {{pause=1000}}

À bientôt {{pause=1000}}
“See you soon!” — even if you don’t know exactly when. {{pause=1000}}

À très vite {{pause=1000}}
This one’s a little more enthusiastic —
like “See you very soon!” {{pause=1000}}

À tout à l’heure ! {{pause=1000}}
Used when you’ll see someone again later today. {{pause=1000}}
You might say this during lunch break —
if you’ll meet again in the afternoon. {{pause=1000}}

À plus tard ! {{pause=1000}}
Also means “see you later” —
but feels a bit more casual and open. {{pause=1000}}

À demain {{pause=1000}}
When you know you’ll see the person tomorrow. {{pause=1000}}
Say it after school, or after work — it’s friendly and clear. {{pause=1000}}

On se voit {{pause=1000}}
Means “we’ll see each other” — like “see you around.” {{pause=1000}}

On se parle {{pause=1000}}
“We’ll talk soon” — this one fits for texting or calling. {{pause=1000}}

À la prochaine ! {{pause=1000}}
“Until next time!” — when you don’t know exactly when you’ll meet. {{pause=1000}}

À la semaine prochaine ! {{pause=1000}}
“See you next week!” — use this for weekly meetups or classes. {{pause=1000}}

Bonne journée ! {{pause=1000}}
Wish someone a “good day” when they’re heading out. {{pause=1000}}

Bonne soirée ! {{pause=1000}}
This is for the evening —
kind and polite, when the sun is setting. {{pause=1000}}
