==========🎤 English Voice (講稿)：==========
In French, there are different ways to say “hello” — depending on the time of day. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}} Salut ! {{pause=1000}}
— This is super casual. Like saying “Hey!” to a friend. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}} Coucou ! {{pause=1000}}
— This one’s even cuter. You say it to close friends, kids, or someone you love. {{pause=500}}
It’s like saying “Hi hi!” or “Peekaboo!” with a smile. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}} Bonjour ! {{pause=1000}}
— Means “Good morning” or just “Hello” during the day. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}} Bon après-midi ! {{pause=1000}}
— This means “Good afternoon,” but it’s not used to greet — it’s more for saying goodbye in the afternoon. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}} Bonsoir ! {{pause=1000}}
— Use this in the evening, like “Good evening.” {{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}} Bonne nuit ! {{pause=1000}}
— Say this only at night, when someone is going to sleep — like “Good night.” {{pause=500}}

==========🎤 English Voice (講稿)：==========

{{pause=1000}}Au revoir{{pause=1000}}
It means “Goodbye” — polite and formal.{{pause=500}}
You use it when saying goodbye to someone you don’t know well {{pause=500}}
like when you're checking out of a hotel, or after paying at a store. {{pause=1000}}
You wouldn’t usually say this to your close friends  {{pause=500}}
they’d just say  {{pause=500}}Salut {{pause=500}} or  {{pause=500}}À plus {{pause=500}} instead. {{pause=500}}

{{pause=500}}Salut !{{pause=1000}}
Hi or bye — friendly and casual. Use it with friends, like saying “Hey!” or “See ya!”{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}À bientôt{{pause=1000}}
It means “See you soon.”{{pause=500}}
Use it when you think you’ll see the person again — maybe in a few days.{{pause=1000}}
For example, after lunch with a friend, you can say:{{pause=500}}
“Thanks for today {{pause=500}}À bientôt!”{{pause=500}}


{{pause=500}}À tout à l’heure{{pause=1000}}
It means “See you later today.”{{pause=500}}
Use it when you’re sure you’ll meet again — like this afternoon.{{pause=1000}}
For example, in the morning at work, you might say:{{pause=500}}
“Let’s talk again after lunch {{pause=500}} À tout à l’heure!”{{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}À plus tard{{pause=1000}}
It's like “Catch you later.”{{pause=800}}
For example, after chatting with your neighbor on the street,
you might say goodbye with a smile:{{pause=500}}“À plus tard !”{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}À demain{{pause=1000}}
See you tomorrow {{pause=300}} when you know you’ll meet the next day, like after class or work.{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}À la prochaine{{pause=1000}}
It means “Until next time.”{{pause=500}}
Great for casual goodbyes — even if you don’t know when you’ll meet again.{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}À la semaine prochaine{{pause=1000}}
It means “See you next week.”{{pause=500}}
Use it if you’ll meet someone again next Monday, in class, or at work.{{pause=500}}

{{pause=1000}}On se voit{{pause=1000}}
It means “See you” {{pause=300}} casual and relaxed.{{pause=500}}
You say it when you’ll probably see the person again soon,{{pause=500}}
like a classmate, coworker, or neighbor. {{pause=500}}
You don’t use it for formal goodbyes — it’s more for everyday situations.{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}On se parle{{pause=1000}}
This means “We’ll talk soon.”{{pause=500}}
You can say it after a text, a call, or a quick chat.{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}Bonne journée{{pause=1000}}
It means “Have a good day.”{{pause=500}}
Use it after buying coffee in the morning {{pause=300}} or when leaving a shop.{{pause=1000}}

{{pause=500}}Bonne soirée{{pause=1000}}
It means “Have a lovely evening.”{{pause=500}}
Say it after dinner, at night, or when leaving an event.{{pause=1000}}
